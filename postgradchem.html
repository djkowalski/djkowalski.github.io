Postgraduate Chemistry Tutorials

The bulk of these tutorials were delivered as problem sessions during my time at the University of Glasgow. There are no 'mark schemes' per se, but comprehensive reference lists are provided the end of each tutorial sheet that contain all the necessary information to answer the questions presented.

  • 1 | Crystal Field Theory
  • 2 | Ligand Field Theory
  • 3 | Ion Pairing
  • 4 | C-H Activation
  • 5 | Radical C-C Formation
  • 6 | UV-vis Spectroscopy

1 | Crystal Field Theory

Covers the fundamentals of Crystal Field Theory using various hydrated metal ions from across the Periodic Table as examples. Questions discuss the geometry, coordination number, splitting of d-orbitals in a crystal field, and rationalising stability based on electron occupation. Consideration is also given to some common distortions from ideal geometries, and some aspects of reactivity.

2 | Ligand Field Theory

Covers the fundamentals of Ligand Field Theory using a variety of organometallic complexes. Questions are constructed around: Covalent Bond Classification and the 18-valence electron (18VE) rule, the effects of selected steric and electronic differences on analytical data, and the rationalisation of both the 18-VE rule and spectrochemical series using an MO diagram for an octahedral complex.


Back to Top

3 | Ion-Pairing and s-Block Reagents

Questions first cover the fundamentals of ion pairing and some practical implications of this in organometallic chemistry. Subsequent questions apply this knowledge to solution-phase aggregation of organolithium complexes, and both the solution-phase structure and mechanism of formation of Grignard reagents. Consideration of the effect of ion pairing on various analytical techniques is highlighted for a number of examples.

4 | C-H Activation with Transition Metal Complexes

Following a primer on organometallic ligands, questions focus on C-H activation with rhodium carbenoids, and C-H insertion of palladium. All examples are drawn from the literature.


Back to Top

5 | Radical Reactions for C-C Bond Formation

Questions are constructed around: estimating the stability of radicals, the impact of stability on selectivity in radical reactions, various named ketyl radical dimerisation reactions (Pinacol, Bouveault-Blanc, McMurry, Acyloin), and selected examples of radical cascade-style transformations in total synthesis.


Back to Top

6 | UV-vis Absorbance Spectroscopy of Transition Metal Complexes

Intended as a primer on the application of UV-vis Absorbance Spectroscopy in traditional coordination chemistry. Questions are constructed around: the types, strength, and energy of electronic transitions, charge transfer processes, term symbols, and using both Orgel and Tanabe-Sugano diagrams to rationalise observed spectra.
