About

Currently in the final year of a PhD in Digital Inorganic Chemistry with the Cronin Group, University of Glasgow. Industrial experience in F. Hoffmann-La Roche Ltd. Keen interest in education and outreach.

Daniel J. Kowalski is a current postgraduate researcher in Digital Chemistry at the University of Glasgow.

He gained an MChem BSc from the University of Leeds in 2018, with a final year project in organometallic chemistry (advisor: Prof Patrick McGowan) and is currently in the final months of a PhD at the University of Glasgow (advisor: Prof Leroy Cronin). During his first degree, he also spent a year as an Intern in Medicinal Chemistry at F. Hoffmann-La Roche Ltd, working on the creation of novel antibiotics with a peptidic component.

He has received various awards for his scientific communication (both spoken and written) from the Royal Society of Chemistry, Canadian Institute of Chemistry, and Society of Chemical Industry, and was awarded the Robert Macrae Prize from the University of Leeds for his Masters research.
