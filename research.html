Research

My interests focus on the applications of data science and digitisation to practical inorganic chemistry. Key themes include: the discovery of novel species; devising strategies for efficient exploration; and developing new digital tools for chemists.

Recent and Selected Projects

See below for a collection of past and present projects. Links to external sites are included as appropriate. Note that descriptions of ongoing projects may be kept vague to protect IP generated.

Empirical Discovery of Binuclear Helicates (2020)

Production of a chemical robot to explore a search space of supramolecular complexes. Two-stage process involved ligand formation (from tandem click reaction and imine condensation) and complexation. My role focused on data analysis and paper writing.

Paper

spectra-visualiser (2021)

Development of a series of wrappers for data visualisation tools in python3 to enable easy generation of attractive figures from raw data formats.

Robotics for Artificial Life (2021)

Design and construction of a robot (based on ChemPU modules) for parallelised exploration of chemical search spaces.

Digital Coordination Chemistry (2022)
