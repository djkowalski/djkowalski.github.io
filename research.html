Research Interests

Most data-driven discovery occurs through screening of chemical space, focusing on structure-property relationships and generation of libraries of structurally-related analogues. My research has focused instead on process space, linking properties to synthetic variables, independent of structure. Work in chemical space often relies heavily on simulation to broaden the searchable space, but simulation is necessarily limited, and practical exploration is still important in exploration focused on the discovery of novelty.

Data Science and Cheminformatics

Process Databases | My key interest in this area is the development and application of databases based on synthetic procedures. Synthetic data is often difficult to access and interpret, and simply constructing these databases is highly challenging. Once created, such datasets have a high potential utility in the development of synthetic routes to new or existing targets amongst other things.

Risk Management | I also have a secondary interest in the practical application of data science to build tools for chemical laboratories. Thus far, this has centred on tools to take advantage of the wealth of safety data freely-available online, with the intention of improving risk quantification and management in chemical inventories. Aims include the rational segregation of highly hazardous substances, ensuring correct storage conditions for sensitive materials, and improving risk management for vulnerable parties (e.g. pregnant people).
