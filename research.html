Research Interests

Exploring for Novelty | Chemical discovery is often focused in chemical space - predicting whether a structure will display a given property (based on intuition or simulation) and then trying to illuminate routes to this target. However, this strategy requires a certain amount of prior data to inform the prediction. I work to design new algorithm-directed strategies to screen process spaces to discover novel substances, without function being used to direct this search. Running experiments without knowing what the product may be introduces further challenges, including engineering serendipity into the exploration, quantifying novelty, and dealing with difficulties arising from attempting to characterise impure systems.

Search Space Design | Some of the challenges of exploring for novelty can be met by intelligent search space design. The choice of which variables can be altered during the exploration, and by how much, is heavily informed by human intuition and heuristics, and this is difficult to quantify and teach to a digital agent. I am trying to codify this into frameworks to enable digital chemists to design their exploration experiments effectively, devise digital strategies to design search spaces, and through clarity, allow the underlying assumptions to be challenged and further probed.

Digital Characterisation and Metric Design | In order to enable closed loop chemical discovery, it is necessary for automated systems to be able to access, transform, and quantify data recorded by typical spectroscopic techniques. I build pipelines to interpret these data sources, and metrics to allow search algorithms to make decisions based on the information contained within. Digital interpretation offers certain advantages over human operators in terms of dataset size and pattern finding, which I have exploited this in the deconvolution of complex system spectra.

Risk Management | Data science has a huge potential in the creation of new tools for chemical laboratories. My work in this area has centred on tools to take advantage of the wealth of safety data freely-available online, with the intention of improving risk quantification and management in chemical inventories. Aims include the rational segregation of highly hazardous substances, ensuring correct storage conditions for sensitive materials, and improving risk management for vulnerable parties (e.g. pregnant people).
